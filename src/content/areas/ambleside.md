## Mild, flat, and quietly icy

Ambleside is the gentlest winter assignment on the North Shore — and that's exactly why properties here get caught out. Strung along Marine Drive at sea level on the harbour, it sees the least snow of anywhere we service. But mild isn't the same as safe. Ocean-moderated air swings temperatures back and forth across 0°C, and the wet pavement from an afternoon of rain refreezes into a thin, near-invisible glaze overnight. A retail strip that never saw a snowflake can still open to an iced-over sidewalk.

That makes Ambleside a [de-icing](/de-icing) and monitoring account first, with [snow clearing](/snow-removal) held in reserve for the handful of real snowfalls each season. We watch conditions on freeze-thaw timing and apply [salt](/salting) as temperatures fall through 0°C — before the glaze forms, not after someone finds it. Our guide on [when to salt a parking lot](/blog/when-to-salt-parking-lot) covers the trigger points we work to.

## Cleared before the shops open

Ambleside is a retail and professional-office district, and those tenants live and die by the morning. There's a narrow window to treat storefront frontage, entrances and lot access before customers, staff and delivery drivers arrive, and hitting it is the difference between a safe opening and a liability. Our crews run that early rotation across the Marine Drive strip and the commercial pockets out toward the [Park Royal](/west-vancouver) periphery, documenting each visit with timestamped photos for the property managers and strata councils behind the frontage.

## Surface-appropriate, corrosion-aware

Much of Ambleside's frontage is decorative or higher-end paving, and the salt-laden harbour air is already hard on it. We favour targeted, surface-appropriate de-icing over blanket rock salt to protect the finish and cut corrosion — the same care that neighbouring [Dundarave](/dundarave) village needs a few blocks west.

The District of West Vancouver requires owners to clear adjacent sidewalks by 10am after a snowfall, and that frontage is mapped into your plan by default. Dispatch is weather-triggered, so crews are moving on the freeze-thaw forecast rather than after your phone rings, and satellite measurement lets us quote most Ambleside frontages same-day without a site visit.
