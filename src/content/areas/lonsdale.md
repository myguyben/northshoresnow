## Our home street, top to bottom

Lonsdale is where North Shore Snow lives — our office sits at #3, 342 Esplanade E in Lower Lonsdale, a block from the Quay. So when we talk about knowing this corridor, it's not a marketing line: it's the street our crews clear first. And Lonsdale is worth knowing, because a single dead-straight avenue climbs from tidewater at the SeaBus terminal all the way up to Grand Boulevard, crossing a real elevation band on the way.

That climb splits the winter. Down at the Quay and along the Esplanade, snow usually arrives as rain and the hazard is the overnight refreeze that glazes the Shipyards plaza and waterfront storefronts. By Central Lonsdale the accumulation starts to hold, and up around Upper Lonsdale and Grand Boulevard you're on genuine snow. We route the corridor accordingly — [de-icing](/de-icing) weighted at the bottom, [plowing](/snow-plowing) and [snow clearing](/snow-removal) weighted at the top.

## The full commercial mix on one corridor

Few streets on the North Shore pack in as much variety. The Shipyards District and Lonsdale Quay bring retail, restaurants and public plazas that need clearing before the morning crowd; Central Lonsdale is thick with office and medical buildings around the Lions Gate Hospital catchment; and the corridor's booming strata towers stack residents, visitor parking and shared walkways into tight footprints. Each type ices differently, so we treat parkade ramps, plaza frontage and tower entrances as distinct priority surfaces rather than one flat lot, with [salting](/salting) triggered as temperatures cross 0°C.

## Walkable means the sidewalk matters

Lonsdale is one of the most-walked corridors on the North Shore, which puts the City of North Vancouver's 10am sidewalk-clearing bylaw front and centre. We map each property's frontage — sidewalks, transit stops and corner cuts — into the service plan so it's cleared on the same pass as the lot, keeping strata councils and property managers compliant and reducing slip-and-fall exposure on a busy public walkway. Our [strata snow removal requirements guide](/blog/strata-snow-removal-requirements-bc) covers what that responsibility involves.
