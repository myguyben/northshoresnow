## Downtown's problem isn't depth — it's ice

Downtown Vancouver almost never sees the accumulation that buries the North Shore benches, and that lulls a lot of buildings into treating winter as a non-event. Then a clear, cold night off the harbour drops temperatures through 0°C, the meltwater on a shaded sidewalk refreezes, and a tower's granite frontage turns into a liability surface before a single flake has fallen. The hazard here is thin, invisible and constant, which makes [de-icing](/de-icing) and monitoring — not plowing — the core of a downtown program.

Tower canyons make it worse. North-facing frontage and the shadow line between highrises stay frozen long after the sun is up elsewhere, and cold-air pooling on parkade ramps keeps them slick well into the morning. We treat those ramps and entrances as their own priority surface, because that's where slips and low-speed vehicle incidents actually happen.

## Cleared before the commute, not after

Downtown work lives or dies on timing. There's a narrow pre-commute window to treat frontage, lobby approaches and ramps before SkyTrain and bus riders, office staff and shoppers arrive. Miss it and you're de-icing around a crowd; hit it and the building is simply safe when the doors open. Our crews run that early-morning rotation on [salting](/salting) and targeted [snow clearing](/snow-removal) when accumulation does arrive, dispatched automatically on weather triggers so nobody's waiting on a phone call at 5am.

Every visit is documented with timestamped photos — the record that protects a property manager when a slip-and-fall claim shows up months later.

## Bylaw compliance for towers and storefronts

The City of Vancouver requires property owners to clear the sidewalks bordering their property by 10am after a snowfall, and downtown frontages are long, busy and highly visible. We build your full tower or retail frontage into the service map so compliance happens on the same early pass as your entrances and ramps. For portfolios juggling multiple buildings, our guide on [when to salt a parking lot](/blog/when-to-salt-parking-lot) covers the trigger points worth knowing.
