## Sea level to snow load in a few blocks

West Vancouver climbs faster than any other part of the North Shore, and its winter splits cleanly by elevation. Along the [Ambleside](/ambleside) and [Dundarave](/dundarave) waterfront on Marine Drive, snow is infrequent and the real danger is the freeze-thaw cycle — ocean-moderated temperatures swing across 0°C and glaze storefronts and lot entrances overnight. A few hundred metres up, the British Properties, Chartwell and Sentinel Hill sit on genuine snow load, holding heavy, wet accumulation long after the waterfront has gone back to rain.

That gap is the whole job here. We monitor elevation-specific conditions rather than a single town forecast, and we pre-position uphill crews before the snow line drops so hillside strata and access roads aren't waiting on trucks fighting their way up. Down at the waterfront, the same storm is handled on freeze-thaw timing with [proactive de-icing](/de-icing) tuned to premium and decorative surfaces.

## Commercial and strata across every elevation

We service Marine Drive retail and restaurant frontage, professional and medical offices, strata complexes on the benches, and commercial properties around the Park Royal periphery and out to [Horseshoe Bay](/horseshoe-bay), where the steep descent into the ferry village ices on the grades. Every program combines [snow clearing](/snow-removal), [plowing](/snow-plowing) and surface-appropriate [salting](/salting) — dispatched on weather triggers and documented with timestamped photos on every visit, which matters when a slip-and-fall claim lands on a strata council's desk.

## Bylaw frontage, handled with the lot

The District of West Vancouver requires owners and occupiers to clear the sidewalks bordering their property by 10am the morning after a snowfall. We map that frontage into your service plan by default, so transit stops, corner cuts and public walkways are cleared on the same pass as your parking lot. For councils weighing what they're actually responsible for, our [strata snow removal requirements guide](/blog/strata-snow-removal-requirements-bc) lays it out.

Seasonal contracts run October 1 to April 30, priced to the property and its elevation rather than a flat rate. Dispatch is weather-triggered, so uphill crews are moving before the snow line drops rather than after the first calls come in, and satellite measurement lets us quote most West Vancouver sites same-day without a site visit.
