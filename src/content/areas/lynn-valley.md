## The North Shore's snowiest commercial ground

If any inhabited part of the North Shore is going to be under snow, it's Lynn Valley. Sitting at the base of the mountains between roughly 150 and 300 metres, it catches the heavy, wet accumulation that a coastal storm barely hints at down at the Quay. When Lonsdale gets rain, Upper Lynn and Braemar are measuring in centimetres — commonly two to three times what falls at sea level, and it sticks around longer because the elevation keeps temperatures down.

That reality drives how we build a Lynn Valley program. This is [plowing](/snow-plowing) and [snow clearing](/snow-removal) country first, backed by [de-icing](/de-icing) — the opposite weighting from the waterfront. We watch elevation-specific conditions rather than a single town forecast and pre-position crews uphill before the snow line drops, because trucks fighting their way up Mountain Highway after the fact is how properties get left waiting.

## Strata, ramps and the freeze that lives at the bottom

Lynn Valley has grown into a dense mix of multi-building strata and townhome complexes around the Lynn Valley Centre commercial core, and those sites share a specific problem: underground parkade ramps. Meltwater runs down them, pools at the bottom and refreezes into sheet ice exactly where a car has the least traction. We treat ramps as a dedicated priority surface with proactive [salting](/salting), alongside the shared walkways and visitor parking that ice at their own pace across a big complex.

Every visit is documented with timestamped photos — the record a strata council or property manager needs when a slip-and-fall claim surfaces. Our [strata snow removal requirements guide](/blog/strata-snow-removal-requirements-bc) breaks down what that responsibility actually covers.
