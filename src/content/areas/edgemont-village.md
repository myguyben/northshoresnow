## Snowy elevation meets a walkable high street

Edgemont Village has a split personality in winter. It sits high at the foot of Grouse Mountain, which makes it one of the snowiest commercial pockets in the entire Greater Vancouver area — heavy, wet snow accumulates fast, and the steep grades feeding into the village turn to ice quickly once it does. At the same time, it's a compact, pedestrian-first retail strip where people park once and walk the block. That combination means the job isn't just moving snow off pavement; it's keeping a busy sidewalk network safe underfoot through the North Shore's heavier end of snowfall.

We build Edgemont programs around that. [Plowing](/snow-plowing) and [snow clearing](/snow-removal) handle the volume the elevation delivers, while [de-icing](/de-icing) on the grades and crossings addresses the ice that follows heavy, wet snow. Because the village depends on foot traffic to function, we service it early in the rotation — a snowed-in high street is a closed one.

## Sidewalks and storefronts come first

On a pedestrian strip, the sidewalk isn't secondary to the parking lot — it's the front line. We prioritise the walkways, corner crossings and storefront entrances along Edgemont Boulevard so shoppers, staff and delivery drivers can move without hitting a glazed patch. That also keeps businesses on the right side of the District of North Vancouver bylaw, which requires the sidewalks bordering a property to be cleared by 10am after a snowfall. Each business's frontage is mapped into the service plan, and [salting](/salting) is applied on the crossings as temperatures fall through 0°C.

## Meticulous where it's visible

Edgemont's retail and professional tenants care how the village looks and feels, so our crews work with the detail a walkable, upscale strip expects — clearing entrances cleanly, keeping walkways passable through a storm, and documenting every visit with timestamped photos for the property managers and strata councils behind the storefronts. For councils sorting out responsibility, our [strata snow removal requirements guide](/blog/strata-snow-removal-requirements-bc) is a useful starting point.
