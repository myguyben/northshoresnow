## Snow removal built for commercial properties

A commercial property can't wait for the plow guy to finish his residential route. Tenants
arrive at 7am, delivery trucks hit the loading dock at 6, and the moment a customer slips
on an uncleared walkway, the cost of "cheap" snow removal becomes very real.

North Shore Snow runs **commercial-first winter operations** across North Vancouver, West
Vancouver and Downtown. Our crews are dispatched automatically on snowfall triggers — by
the time your first employee arrives, the lot is plowed, walkways are clear, and the
entrance is salted.

## What a service visit includes

Every snow event follows the same sequence: drive aisles and parking areas are plowed
first for access, sidewalks and entrances are cleared by walkway crews, high-traffic
pedestrian routes get de-icing treatment, and the visit is closed out with GPS-stamped
photos uploaded to your property file. If accumulation continues, crews return on
rotation until the storm ends — that's included in seasonal contracts, not billed as an
extra.

## Why response time is the whole game

The North Shore's freeze-thaw cycle punishes slow contractors. Snow that sits through a
2°C afternoon becomes ice by 6pm, and ice is ten times the liability of snow. Our routing
puts crews on your property while snow is still soft — faster to clear, cheaper on
materials, and dramatically safer. That's also why we pair every clearing program with
[proactive de-icing](/de-icing): treating surfaces before the refreeze is what actually keeps
your property safe.

## Documented service, defensible records

BC courts assess winter slip-and-fall claims on whether the property owner took
*reasonable care*. "The contractor usually comes" is not a defence. A time-stamped photo
record showing your property was cleared and salted at 5:42am — that's a defence. Every
North Shore Snow visit generates one automatically. Read more in our guide to
[slip-and-fall liability for BC commercial property](/blog/slip-and-fall-liability-commercial-property-bc).
