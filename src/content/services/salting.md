## Calibrated salting, not shovel-and-guess

Over-salting a parking lot is easy — and it's a problem, not a safety margin. Too much salt
tracks into buildings, pits concrete, corrodes vehicles, kills the landscaping come spring,
and burns money for no extra traction. North Shore Snow applies salt from calibrated
spreaders set to the rate the surface and temperature actually call for. The goal is
complete coverage of every critical surface with the least material that does the job —
that's what "calibrated" means, and it's the difference between a lot that's genuinely safe
and one that's just white.

## The right material for the temperature

Not all ice melt works at all temperatures. Standard rock salt (sodium chloride) is the
workhorse for commercial lots and melts reliably down to about -12°C — which covers most of
what the North Shore throws at a property. When the mercury drops below roughly -10°C, plain
rock salt slows down, so we switch to treated and blended materials that keep melting in
deeper cold. Matching material to the forecast is why our salting actually clears ice
instead of sitting on top of it. It's the granular counterpart to the
[liquid anti-icing in our de-icing program](/de-icing).

## Sidewalks, entrances and the 10am bylaw

Most slip-and-fall claims don't happen in the middle of the lot — they happen at the curb,
the entrance and the sidewalk, where people are getting in and out of cars. Every salting
visit treats those pedestrian pinch points: building entrances, walkways, transit-stop
frontage, and the ramps and stairs in between. North Shore municipal bylaws require property
frontage sidewalks cleared and safe by 10am after a snowfall, and entrance treatment is how
you actually hit that mark. It pairs directly with our [snow plowing](/snow-plowing) and
[full snow removal](/snow-removal) service, so the whole property — drive lanes to doorstep
— gets covered on one visit.

## Right-sizing is the environmental answer

Salt runoff ends up in North Shore creeks and storm drains, and the honest way to reduce it
isn't to under-treat a lot and hope — it's to apply exactly the right amount and no more.
Right-sized, calibrated application is both the safer practice and the greener one: less
chloride in the watershed, less damage to soil and vegetation, and a lot that's still fully
treated. For sensitive sites near waterways we can work in treated grit for traction where
heavy salting isn't the right call.

## A record of every application

Every salting run is logged with GPS-stamped, timestamped photos filed to your property
record, so you can prove exactly what was treated and when — the documentation that defends
a slip-and-fall claim and keeps a portfolio audit-ready. Standalone salting dispatches
automatically whenever temperatures reach 0°C, and it's built into every plow visit as well.
