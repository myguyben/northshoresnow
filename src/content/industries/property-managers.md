## One contract, every site in your portfolio

Property managers do not have a snow problem — they have a coordination problem. Ten sites,
ten layouts, ten sets of priorities, and a winter that hits them all at once. North Shore
Snow manages your whole portfolio under one agreement, with each property individually mapped
from satellite imagery, scoped and priced. Driveways, [snow removal](/snow-removal) routes,
[de-icing](/de-icing) and [salting](/salting) requirements, and municipal sidewalk frontage
are set per site before the season starts, so nothing is improvised in a storm. You manage
one relationship and one renewal, not a directory of contractors. Adding a property
mid-season is a single email, not a new procurement cycle, and its scope and pricing follow
the same measured method as the rest of your portfolio.

## Reporting your owners will actually accept

The value you add is accountability, and accountability needs evidence. Every visit we make
is GPS-logged with time-stamped photos, filed by property and date. When an owner asks whether
their building was cleared, you forward a record instead of relaying a promise. When an
insurer wants winter-maintenance history for a renewal, it already exists, organized and
exportable per site.

## Consolidated invoicing, per-site detail

Winter billing should not cost you a day of reconciliation. We consolidate charges into a
single statement with a clean per-site breakdown, so you can allocate costs, pass them
through to owners, and close the month without chasing paper. Seasonal pricing means each
site carries one predictable figure, whatever the weather does — no per-storm surprises to
explain to a council.

## Response SLAs and a real escalation path

The reason a tenant's slip becomes your emergency is that most contractors have no plan for
it. We do. Dispatch is triggered by weather monitoring, response-time targets are written
into your contract per property, and you get a named contact who answers when it matters.
