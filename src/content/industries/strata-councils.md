## You are the one who has to defend the decision

When a strata council signs a snow contract, the council members answer for it — at the
next AGM, in the budget line owners scrutinize, and if someone slips and the corporation
is named in a claim. This page is written for the people doing the choosing, not only for
the property being serviced. For the property-level detail on how service runs day to day,
see our [strata properties](/strata) page.

## Compare quotes apples-to-apples

The hardest part of choosing a snow contractor is that no two quotes describe the same
thing. Every North Shore Snow quote comes with a satellite-measured service map — the exact
driveways, walkways, visitor stalls, parkade ramps and municipal sidewalk frontage included,
with measured areas. Put it beside a competitor's quote and the gaps are obvious: if their
number is lower because they left out the ramp, you see it before you sign, not in February.
Make sure any quote covers both clearing and ice control — [snow removal](/snow-removal),
[de-icing](/de-icing) and [salting](/salting) are distinct line items, and a quote that omits
ice control will cost you mid-season.

## What to ask before you sign

Ask every contractor you are considering the same short list, and get the answers in writing:

- Is dispatch triggered by weather monitoring, or do we have to phone during the storm?
- What is included at the quoted price, and what gets billed as extra?
- Do we receive a record of each visit — arrival times and photos?
- Does the seasonal price hold no matter how many times it snows?
- Who carries the liability, and what documentation backs a claim defence?

Our guide to [what strata snow removal requires under BC law](/blog/strata-snow-removal-requirements-bc)
walks through these obligations in more depth.

## The Strata Property Act duty is yours

Under the Strata Property Act the corporation must maintain common property, and courts
assessing a slip-and-fall look for a *system* of reasonable, documented winter maintenance.
A contract with weather-triggered dispatch and photo-verified visit records is precisely that
kind of system. Our overview of
[slip-and-fall liability for commercial property in BC](/blog/slip-and-fall-liability-commercial-property-bc)
covers what adjudicators actually weigh.
