## Ready before the doors open

Retail runs on a clock, and the clock is your opening hour. A parking lot still being plowed
when the first customers pull in is lost sales, frustrated shoppers and a property that looks
closed. North Shore Snow schedules each site around when you open, and because dispatch is
triggered by weather monitoring rather than a phone call, crews are clearing the lot, entries
and walkways before your doors do. [Snow removal](/snow-removal) and [de-icing](/de-icing) are
run as one coordinated plan, sized to your centre from satellite measurement so the quote
reflects your real serviceable area.

## Parking that keeps turning over

For a shopping centre, parking is inventory — every stall that is buried or blocked is a sale
that does not happen. We map clearing routes and snow-stacking areas so aisles stay drivable
and stalls stay usable through accumulation, and we stack snow where it will not swallow your
best spaces. Keeping the lot flowing during a storm is what keeps customers arriving and
leaving.

## Frontage, entries and cart corrals

The surfaces customers actually touch get first priority. Storefront frontage, entrances,
crosswalks and cart corrals are mapped as pedestrian zones and treated with [salting](/salting)
and ice melt on a weather-driven schedule. A clean, welcoming frontage keeps the property
reading as open, and keeps foot traffic on its feet. Between visits we return as conditions
warrant rather than clearing once and calling it done, because a centre that was clear at
8am and glazed over by noon is the one that ends up defending a claim.

## Foot traffic multiplies your liability

Hundreds or thousands of shoppers a day means the odds of a slip-and-fall are simply higher
than at a quiet property, and the claim lands on the centre. Frequent, consistent ice control
lowers the risk, and GPS-stamped photo records of every visit give you the evidence to defend
the property if a claim is filed. Our overview of
[slip-and-fall liability for commercial property in BC](/blog/slip-and-fall-liability-commercial-property-bc)
explains what actually holds up.
